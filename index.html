What Is NAEYC?

Think of It as a Gold Seal for Preschools

NAEYC stands for the National Association for the Education of Young Children. It's the largest and most respected organization in early childhood education. Earning NAEYC recognition is voluntary. A school has to choose to go through a rigorous evaluation process, and only about 8% of preschools nationwide meet the bar.

Here is what it means in practice for your child:

✓
Qualified, trained teachers Our educators meet national standards for education, experience, and ongoing professional development.
✓
Research-backed curriculum Learning activities are designed for how young children actually learn, not just what looks impressive on paper.
✓
Safe, healthy environment Health, safety, and supervision standards are verified by outside assessors, not just self-reported.
✓
Unannounced inspections NAEYC sends assessors for random, surprise visits to make sure standards are maintained every day, not just on evaluation day.

Most Vienna-area part-time preschools, including county programs and other co-ops, do not have NAEYC recognition. Meekins does.

What Is a Co-Op Preschool?

A cooperative preschool is a unique model where families play an active role in the school community. Parents volunteer in the classroom alongside professional teachers, participate in school governance, and help shape the programs their children enjoy. It's a partnership between families and educators that creates a richer, more connected experience for everyone.

The Co-Op Life

More Than a Preschool - A Partnership

At Meekins, the cooperative model means you are not just dropping off your child - you are part of their educational journey. You will see their "aha" moments firsthand, build relationships with other families, and gain parenting insights from our expert teachers. Here are answers to the most common questions:

“Will I have time to volunteer?”

Co-op duties are shared among all families and are designed to be manageable. Most parents assist in the classroom just a few times per month, and there are flexible options to fit different schedules. Many families find that the time they spend in the classroom becomes the highlight of their week.

“What if I work full-time?”

Many Meekins families have two working parents. Grandparents, caregivers, or other trusted adults can fulfill classroom duties on your behalf. There are also non-classroom roles - like fundraising, event planning, or communications - that can be done on your own schedule.

“I'm not a teacher - will I know what to do?”

Absolutely! Our lead teachers guide everything. Your role as a classroom helper is clearly defined, and you will receive orientation and support. You do not need any teaching experience - just a willingness to be present and engaged.
